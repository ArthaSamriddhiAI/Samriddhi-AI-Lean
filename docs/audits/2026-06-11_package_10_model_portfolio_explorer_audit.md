## Part A: grounded findings

### A1. Where the model-portfolio specification actually lives, and what it is

The kickoff's caution is confirmed and then some: the rich two-layer specification is real, locked, and entirely external to this codebase, and it was authored for the full Samriddhi cluster architecture, not the Lean MVP.

**A1.1 The two-layer tagging plus preferred-portfolio architecture.** [audited] FR Entry 13.0 ("Model Portfolio Architecture Overview", status "Locked (cluster 4)", April 2026) specifies: Layer One, exhaustive tagging of every Instrument record with client-profile cell tags ("all 1300+ instruments are tagged", a JSON array field on the Instrument canonical entity per FR 13.1); Layer Two, a `PreferredPortfolioEntry` canonical entity per FR 13.2 (one instrument in one cell with role core/satellite/optional and rank within role, roughly 60 to 100 default entries across 9 cells per FR 13.3); default loaders, two CIO editing surfaces, T1 telemetry events, and snapshot capture. All of this presumes the full product's cluster stack (canonical entities from cluster 3, T1 telemetry, CIO role, snapshot machinery), none of which exists in the Lean repo.

**A1.2 The nine-cell matrix.** [audited] FR 13.0 section 3: a 3x3 (risk_profile, horizon) grid with the cell enum `aggressive|moderate|conservative` x `long_term|medium_term|short_term` and a direct mapping from investor `risk_appetite` and `time_horizon` fields. The original team Excel (`19 - Model Portfolio/Model_Portfolio.xlsx`) used a 3x3x4 matrix with an investable-amount bracket dimension that FR 13.0 section 3.2 explicitly drops for the HNI focus. [hypothesis: the xlsx contents are characterised from FR 13.0's description; the file was not parsed in this pass.]

**A1.3 The L1/L2/L3 drift framework.** [audited] The L1/L2/L3 drift framework lives in the **scrapped** consolidation v1 (`02 - Change Implementation v1 - Scrapped/consolidated - 01 - samriddhi_consolidation_v1.md`): L1 asset-class drift with action-triggering tolerance bands (default plus or minus 5 percent of the bucket's L1 target), L2 vehicle-mix drift (informational), L3 sub-asset-class drift (informational), action trigger at L1 only. The successor cluster plan does not carry it forward as built work: the Cluster 4 demo-stage addendum states "No Drift Monitoring" and defers drift to cluster 17+, noting "The L2 (vehicle mix) and L3 (sub-asset class) guidance is implicit in the preferred portfolio's curation". So even on the reference side, the drift framework is one generation more parked than the kickoff's phrasing implies.

**A1.4 The Lean foundation explicitly excludes the two-layer architecture.** [audited] `foundation/foundation.md` ships one indicative model portfolio, the `aggressive_long_term` cell of "the broader 3x3 model portfolio matrix" (line 35), with the 65/25/7/3 split, bands, equity sub-allocation, and vehicle mix (lines 41 to 72), and the out-of-scope section states: "The MVP ships with the single indicative model portfolio in Section 2. CIO editing surfaces, tag editing, preferred portfolio curation, and the broader two-layer architecture are out of scope" (line 472). The external source foundation (`02 - Factual Foundation/Lean_Samriddhi_MVP_Factual_Foundation.md`, line 450) says the same.

**Conclusion for A1**: the two-layer architecture, nine-cell matrix, and L1/L2/L3 drift framework are locked reference for the full product (and partially scrapped reference), and are excluded-by-design from the Lean MVP foundation. Anything the package builds from them is new build against P43, not restoration of something the codebase once had.

### A2. The instrument tagging layer: does not exist as data, anywhere on the shipped store

[audited, verified directly in this pass, not carried from any summary]

- A recursive key walk over the committed t0 snapshot (`fixtures/snapshots/enriched/snapshot_t0_q2_2026.json`, resolved through the documented symlink to the data repo clone, data repo ADR-0003) collected 794 distinct key names across all sections. Keys matching `tag|cell|prefer|suitab|approv|recommend` (case-insensitive): exactly one, `business_model_tag`, on `unlisted_equity[].classification`, a descriptive company-business-model label ("B2C"), not an appropriateness or curation flag.
- Instrument counts on t0: 1,773 `mf_funds`, 500 `nifty500.companies`, 513 `pms.funds`, 162 `aif["Fund Profiles"]`, 100 `unlisted_equity`. **Cell-tagged instruments: 0 of all of them. Fraction of the universe tagged: 0%.**
- There is no `Instrument` table at all: `prisma/schema.prisma` defines `Investor`, `Snapshot` (metadata only, JSONs on disk), `Case`, `Setting`. The instrument universe is disk JSON reachable through `lib/agents/snapshot-loader.ts` (`loadSnapshot`, `loadSnapshotPair`, LRU capacity 3), never a queryable DB entity.
- The exact identifier `PreferredPortfolioEntry` occurs nowhere in this repo, the data repo, the design bundles, or any reference folder; its only occurrence in the entire project tree is the Package 10 kickoff itself. The concept exists as FR 13.2 (A1.1); the name does not exist as an artifact.

### A3. The preferred best-pick layer: no curated layer exists; a computed funnel ships instead

[audited]

- **No curation layer**: no table, no fixture, no type, no editing surface for per-cell preferred entries (greps across `lib/`, `db/`, `app/`, `components/`, `prisma/`, `scripts/`, `docs/`).
- **What ships is the deterministic instrument-selection funnel** (`lib/agents/instrument-selection.ts`, 652 lines; mechanics ADR-0034, allocation framework ADR-0033/0035/0036/0037): eligibility floors (AUM at least 500 Cr, age at least 3y, complete 3y risk stats; `SELECTION_PARAMS`, lines 30 to 56), risk-adjusted composite cohort (Sharpe/Sortino/Calmar average rank, top half), lowest-TER ranking with 3y-return tiebreak, surface 3 of internal 5, honest degradation on thin pools (`runFunnelOnPool`, lines 286 to 305). Entry point `buildDeploymentPlan` (line 635) emits `SleeveDeploymentPlan[]` covering equity sub-buckets (international, domestic large/mid/small, plus a diversified-equity option), debt 2D credit-by-duration cells, alternatives (gold shortlist, non-gold advisor-select), and held-fund top-up assessments.
- **Real persisted instances exist**: all five Samriddhi 2 case fixtures carry `content.a3_so_what.deployment_plan` (Bhatt 1 sleeve, Iyengar 1, Malhotra 1, Menon 3, Surana 2; verified by reading the fixtures). Menon's plan, for example, surfaces per-sub-bucket shortlists with named funds and cadence (international: Navi Total Stock Market US FOF and peers; domestic large: ICICI Prudential Large Cap and peers; debt sovereign: ICICI Prudential Constant Maturity Gilt; gold: Mirae Asset Gold ETF).
- **Semantics differ from the FR 13.2 layer**: the funnel is computed quality-gated selection under named house-view parameters (calibration pending, P45), not CIO curation with core/satellite/optional roles. There are no roles, no rank-within-role, no per-cell curated lists, no governance events. It answers "what would the firm deploy into this under-target sleeve for this investor" deterministically; it does not answer "what has the CIO curated for cell X".

### A4. The nine profile cells: which are live

[audited]

- `Investor.modelCell` is a free `String` column (`prisma/schema.prisma:23`). Seeded values: `aggressive_long_term` for five investors (sharma, bhatt, malhotra, menon, surana), `conservative_medium_term` for Iyengar (`db/seed.ts:347-422`). The onboarding flow writes the sentinel `"pending_mandate"`, which is not a cell at all (`app/investors/onboard/actions.ts:141`). So **2 of 9 cells have instances; 7 have never been instantiated**, and newly onboarded investors currently leave the cell unassigned.
- **No cell-keyed band grid exists.** P43 (`docs/debt/product_debt_log.md`) records it exactly and the code confirms it: the per-investor mandates in `MANDATES_BY_INVESTOR` (`db/fixtures/structured-mandates.ts:142-264`) are bespoke and hand-authored; Bhatt, Sharma, and Menon carry the identical cell tag yet three different band sets. The tags annotate; they do not drive.
- **What the framework actually keys on is risk tier and horizon, not cells**: `resolveHhiTier(riskAppetite)` maps the stated risk-appetite string to one of four tiers (Conservative, Moderate-Aggressive, Aggressive, Ultra-Aggressive; `lib/agents/portfolio-risk-analytics.ts:245-252`); `EQUITY_SPLIT_BY_TIER` and `DEBT_CREDIT_SPLIT_BY_TIER` carry house-view sub-sleeve splits per tier; `durationForHorizon` maps horizon text to a debt duration bucket (`db/fixtures/structured-mandates.ts:95-117`); `resolveFramework(tier, horizon, override)` composes them with a per-investor `sub_sleeve_tilt` override seam (`lib/agents/instrument-selection.ts:98-102`). That is a live 4-tier-by-3-duration framework, explicitly documented in-file as "the usable foundation of a model portfolio, built to be extended (not replaced) when the full risk-appetite-by-time-horizon framework is formalised (product debt P43)" (`db/fixtures/structured-mandates.ts:51-58`).
- **The single-indicative-cell MVP simplification: still present, but evolved.** The `aggressive_long_term` reference survives in three places: the foundation section 2 register, the `MODEL_BANDS` constants (`lib/agents/portfolio-risk-analytics.ts:28-33`), and the `model_cell` string passed into agent prompt context (`lib/agents/pipeline.ts:214,277`). But since ADR-0032 the operative comparison is mandate-first: `resolveTargetBands(mandate)` resolves each investor's own bands with explicit-target-or-midpoint, falling back to `MODEL_BANDS` only when no mandate exists (`portfolio-risk-analytics.ts:54-68`). All six seeded investors carry mandates. The foundation section 6 informational reframe for non-aggressive investors is implemented in case content (Iyengar's framing line: "Direct model-portfolio comparison is informational for a conservative-mandate investor", `db/fixtures/cases/c-2026-05-15-iyengar-01.json:186`). So: single cell as reference and fallback, per-investor mandates as the operative comparator.
- **"Risk appetite as practiced": no derived classifier exists.** The kickoff frames the matrix axis as practiced risk appetite "derived from the investor profile and allocation". Nothing in the codebase derives a risk tier from allocation: `resolveHhiTier` reads the stated string only. What exists instead is divergence machinery: the `stated_revealed_divergence` observation vocabulary (`lib/agents/a3-so-what.ts:676`, S1/A2 vocab), Sharma's mandate-set "moderate-aggressive (closer to moderate by revealed pattern)" hand-authoring (foundation section 4), and the A3 `advisor_framing_note` (stated-versus-observed framing, T-5.12 Finding 3). Practiced-versus-stated is a surfaced observation, not a computed cell assignment.

### A5. Allocation-target and band data, and drift logic

[audited]

**Structured and real today:**

- `MODEL_BANDS` (single-cell targets and bands), concentration constants (position 10/15, sector 25/35, wrapper count 4, wrapper share 25, HHI ceilings by tier 0.20/0.25/0.30/0.35), liquidity bucket map (`portfolio-risk-analytics.ts:26-133`).
- Per-investor mandate bands with optional explicit `target_pct` (ADR-0032), wrapper-count and concentration ceilings, exclusions (`structured-mandates.ts`), serialised to `Investor.mandateJson` at seed, read by G1 and the metrics layer.
- The tier-keyed sub-sleeve splits and the funnel parameters (A3, A4 above).
- Per-case persisted band arithmetic: `content.metrics.assetClass` carries actual/target/band/deviation/inBand per asset class per investor, plus the whole concentration, liquidity, and cash-deployment blocks (PortfolioMetrics, `portfolio-risk-analytics.ts:136-220`, persisted in all five Samriddhi 2 fixtures).

**Two allocation vocabularies coexist, and only one is consumed by code.** The foundation section 2 equity sub-allocation (large 50, flexi 20, mid 15, small 5, international 10, as percent of equity) and vehicle mix (MF active 40, MF passive 10, PMS 10, direct 5, as percent of AUM) exist as prose and as the designed Section A table (`screen_explorer.jsx:60-141`); no code reads them. The shipped sub-sleeve framework is the ADR-0033 tier-keyed one with different numbers (Aggressive: international 20 percent of the equity sleeve; domestic split 35/40/25 across large/mid/small). A model-portfolio surface that renders both without labelling them as distinct registers would imply a coherence the codebase does not have (see RS2).

**Drift logic**: no L1/L2/L3 tiered drift framework exists in code under that name or mechanics (the only L1/L2/L3 in the repo is the unrelated portfolio-overlap layer naming, ADR-0030). What exists: per-class band deviation with in/out-of-band flags (metrics), the `allocation_drift` observation vocabulary (S1/A2/A3), sub-bucket gap math inside the funnel (target versus current per equity sub-bucket and debt cell), and the G1 mandate-compliance gate. The plus-or-minus-5-percent action-trigger framework is reference-only, in a scrapped doc (A1.3).

## Part B: proposal to implement, in two separately ratifiable parts

### Part (a): render-existing (ratify to build as-is; wireframed)
